The Ripple Effect

Every action has consequences that reach beyond ourselves. Let's identify who experienced those effects.

Restorative Action (Immediate Action)

Harmful actions are repairable. In this section you will decide how to repair the harm caused by your actions.
Relationship Repair
▼
Choose this if a person was hurt, disrespected, or negatively affected by your words or actions.
The Goal: To rebuild trust and show the other person that you value them.
Examples: You said something mean or argued with a teacher.
What actions can I take to repair the harm and rebuild trust right now?
Environment Repair
▼
Choose this if your actions disrupted the whole class, made it hard for others to learn, or physically messed up the classroom.
The Goal: To give back to the group and help the classroom run smoothly again.
Examples: You were blurting out during a lesson, left a mess at your desk, or were wandering the room.
What actions can I take to repair the harm and rebuild trust right now?
Self Repair
▼
Choose this if you feel like you "lost control" of your choices, or if your behavior mostly hurt your own progress and reputation.
The Goal: To figure out why it happened and build a "shield" so it doesn't happen again.
Examples: You refused to do your work, put your head down, or keep finding yourself in the same trouble over and over.
What actions can I take to repair the harm and rebuild trust right now?
